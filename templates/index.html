System overview

Welcome to our system!
Here's how we operate to provide you with the best music experience:
1. Data Collection - First and foremost, we collect data on songs and users' music preferences. (format .csv)
2. Data Processing and Cleaning - The collected data then undergoes a processing and cleaning procedure to ensure quality and reliability.
3. Data Splitting - After cleaning, the data is divided into two parts: a training dataset and an evaluation dataset (test). The training portion is used to 'teach' the model, while the evaluation part helps us assess the model's performance.
4. Model Training - Utilizing the training dataset, we train a recommendation model. This model learns from the data to provide suggestions tailored to your music taste.
5. API Deployment - Once the model is trained and tested, we deploy it as an API, enabling seamless integration into applications and offering recommendation services.
6. Heroku Deployment - We use the Heroku platform to deploy our application. Heroku makes it easy for us to deploy, manage, and maintain the application without much technical complexity.
All these steps are geared towards providing you with the best music listening experience. We hope you enjoy and discover songs that perfectly match your musical preferences!"

Collaborative Filtering (CF)

- Collaborative Filtering helps recommend songs based on the behavior and preferences of other users.
- For each song that the user has listened to, the collaborative filtering model predicts the user's interest in unlistened songs by comparing it with other users who have similar preferences.
- Result: A list of songs ranked based on the user's likelihood of interest, considering other users.

Content-Based Filtering (CBF)

- Collaborative Filtering helps recommend songs based on the behavior and preferences of other users.
- For each song that the user has listened to, the collaborative filtering model predicts the user's interest in unlistened songs by comparing it with other users who have similar preferences.
- Result: A list of songs ranked based on the user's likelihood of interest, considering other users.

Hybrid Recommendations

- To generate the final result, both results are combined.
- A simple combining function, possibly a simple average or a weight, is applied to the results from each model.
- Songs that the user has listened to are removed from the final recommended list to ensure uniqueness
The final result is a list of songs ranked based on the combination of both methods. The way each model contributes to the recommendation list is adjusted through their combination, and songs that the user has listened to before will not appear in the list to ensure uniqueness. Collaborative Filtering and Content-Based Filtering, working together harmoniously, bring you a tailored list of songs to elevate your music experience. Enjoy the tunes!
